Missile Man Of India

Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam, the Former President of India and a world-renowned Space Scientist

Here's a time line of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  • Born in 1931 at Rameswaram in the state of Tamil Nadu, India.
  • In 1954, he graduated in Physics from St. Joseph's College in Tiruchirappalli.
  • From 1963 to 1964, he visited the Goddard Space Flight Center in Greenbelt
  • In 1965, he worked independently in Defense Research and Development Organisation for the first time on an expandable rocket project
  • In July 1980, his team was successful in deploying the Rohini satellite near the orbit of the Earth
  • Dr. Kalam's efforts in developing the projects on SLV-III and Polar SLV from 1970s to 1990s proved to be successful.
  • From July 1992 to December 1999 he remained the Secretary of the Defense Research and Development Organisation
  • He developed a low-cost Coronary Stent along with Dr. Soma Raju, a cardiologist, in 1998.
  • The National Democratic Alliance (NDA) government on 10 June 2002 proposed Dr. Kalam's name for the Presidential post to the Leader of Opposition, Congress President Sonia Gandhi.
  • Dr. Kalam served as the President of India from 25 July 2002 to 25 July 2007.
  • Dr. Kalam succeeded K.R. Narayanan as the 11th President of India.
  • The United Nations has recognized Dr. Kalam's 79th birthday as "World Student's Day".
  • He received the Bharat Ratna in 1997 for his contribution in the field of scientific research, development and modernisation of technology in the defense sector of India.
